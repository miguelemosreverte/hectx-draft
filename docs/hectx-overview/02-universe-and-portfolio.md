**Abstract**
This chapter defines the investable universe, eligibility constraints, and portfolio construction principles that underpin NAV and portfolio deployment. It translates HectX’s selection criteria and diversification goals into implementable policy objects.

**Body**
I. Investable Universe (High-Level)
I.a The universe is a curated set of private, pre-IPO companies with late-stage scale and growth characteristics.
I.b Companies are accessed through private markets using a blend of primary and secondary transactions.

II. Eligibility Criteria
II.a Companies must be private, pre-IPO, and have strong legal standing, governance practices, and available information for valuation.
II.b Companies must meet Hecto’s financial and operational criteria, and be eligible to be acquired lawfully via private-market transactions.
II.c Hecto can exclude companies based on legal, regulatory, reputational, or strategic concerns.

III. Selection And Due Diligence
III.a Multi-factor assessment includes market position, financial performance, governance, and exit potential.
III.b A combination of internal analysis and external valuation inputs is used to determine inclusion and sizing.

IV. Portfolio Construction
IV.a Diversification targets across sectors, stages, and geographies.
IV.b Concentration limits and maximum position sizing are enforced to manage idiosyncratic risk.
IV.c A reserve sleeve is maintained for subscriptions and liquidity management.

V. Candidate Universe Snapshot
V.a The documentation includes a non-exhaustive list of 30 candidate companies as of March 10, 2025.
V.b Example candidates include: Epic Games, Figma, Databricks, Stripe, SpaceX, Anduril, Revolut, and others.
V.c The list is illustrative and does not guarantee holdings or allocations.
