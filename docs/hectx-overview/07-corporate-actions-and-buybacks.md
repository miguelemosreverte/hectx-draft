**Abstract**
Corporate actions of underlying portfolio companies change NAV and portfolio composition. Hecto may hold, sell, or rebalance positions following liquidity events, with proceeds retained by the vehicle and allocated per policy.

**Body**
I. Corporate Action Types (High-Level)
I.a IPOs and direct listings.
I.b M&A events, tender offers, or secondary liquidity events.

II. NAV And Portfolio Effects
II.a Valuation updates are required when a portfolio company becomes public or is acquired.
II.b Hecto may hold public shares, sell down, or exit based on policy, lockups, and market conditions.
II.c Proceeds are retained in the vehicle and may be reallocated to reserves, reinvested, or used for buybacks.

III. Distribution And Buybacks
III.a HectX does not guarantee redemption rights.
III.b Distributions are not automatic; discretionary buybacks may be executed at Hecto’s discretion.

IV. Operational Workflow
IV.a Detect corporate action event and verify terms.
IV.b Update valuation inputs and NAV.
IV.c Decide allocation of proceeds and adjust portfolio weights.
